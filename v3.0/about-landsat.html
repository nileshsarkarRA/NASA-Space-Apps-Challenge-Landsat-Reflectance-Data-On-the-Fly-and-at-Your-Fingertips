About Landsat

Landsat is a long-running Earth observation satellite program jointly managed by NASA and the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS). It provides continuous global coverage of the Earth's surface in multispectral imagery, allowing for the monitoring and analysis of land use, vegetation, water resources, and other environmental factors.

Landsat data has been used for a wide range of applications, including:

  • Forest monitoring and deforestation detection
  • Agricultural land use analysis
  • Disaster response and recovery
  • Climate change research
  • Urban planning and development

Learn more about Landsat and its capabilities on the official NASA and USGS websites.
